Mid-War: Major Naval Battles

Pearl Harbor

On December 7th, 1941; Pearl Harbor was attacked by the Imperial Japanese Navy Air Service
In the Battle thousands of sailors were either injured or killed along with many of the American Navy
ships destroyed. This event prompted the United States to declare war on the Axis and on Japan.

American Advantages

America Begins The Battle with Doolittle Raid aka The Tokyo Raid. At April 18, 1942, was a air raid by the U.S.A
on Tokyo and on the islands of Honshu. The Americans soon realized that they had the advantage
of mainland vulnerability against American air raids. Resulting in a boost of American morale.

The Battle of Midway

The Battle Of Midway occured 6 months after Pearl Harbor with a American Victory against the Japanese
allowing America and its allies to move into a offensive position in the Pacific Campaign.
The Guadalcanal Campaign took place from November 12th to November 15th of 1942 resulting
into a American Strategic and decisive victory.

Battle of Philippine Sea

The Battle of Philippine Sea was a extreme naval battle eliminating the Imperial Japanese Navy's
ability to conduct large-scale carrier actions happening in the invasion of the mariana Islands limiting the Japanese.

Battle Of Iwo Jima: Naval Aspect

The Battle of Iwo Jima began in February 1945 with the need of a base near the
Japanese coast. After many bombings from both air and naval leading to marine divisions to board the
island having difficulty pushing through the 23,000 Japanese Troops and after the battle it was
a American victory.
