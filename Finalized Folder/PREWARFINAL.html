Pre-war: The Powderkeg of Europe

The Rise of Fascism and Militarism.

The Humiliation of Versailles

In 1919 Treaty of Versailles is signed. Germany suffers economic, military and territorial
consequence. Some of which is major territorial loss to France and Poland coupled
with the huge amount of war reparations put on them. The amount of reparations is impossible to pay, especially with a recovering economy.
Soon after the war German economy collapses into further debt while the Allies occupy the Ruhr, a rich region in Germany
and without the Ruhr Germany falls even further into debt! Germany at this point is in the worst economic disaster they could imagine.
Over 6 million are on the streets and they are all hopeless!

The Rise of Fascism

Italy was also under hard times. Though they did not retain democracy as they were taken over by Benito Mussolini and his Fascist party.
Mussolini promises to bring back the greatness of the Roman Empire. Italy and other nations used a sense of nationalism to further their
efforts and ambitions. Going back to Germany, in 1933 Germany was in political turmoil with Communists on one side and Hitlers Fascists on the other
and in a landslide victory, Hitler wins the chancellor elections.

Hitler Seizes Absolute Power

Not very long after Hitler is elected Chancellor, the President of Germany Hindenburg dies and Hitler uses his immense power in German Parliament
to merge the position of President and Chancellor into one leadership role, "Der Fuhrer" or The Leader in German is the name of this role. Hitler also
uses his power to blame a terrorist attack on the government building on the Communist party of Germany, the KPD or Roterfront(Red Front in German)
He uses this as an excuse to ban all other parties except the Nazi party and to suspend elections indefinetly. Hitlers absolute rule has begun.

German Rearmament and Expansion

Soon after Hitler seizes absolute power, he orders the massive rearmament of the German army, navy and airforce. This rearmament goes against the
Treaty of Versailles but Britain and France do not to anything about it. Hitler also puts the military back into the Rhineland. The Allies are alarmed
and make an alliance but do not do anything. Then Germany annexes Austria and the Allies to not contest. They soon take the Sudetenland of Czechslovakia
and a war almost happens this time as the Allies final put their foot down. They have Hitler promise not to invade any more countries or there will be war.
Hitler promises, but breaks his promise shortly after and invades the rest of Czechoslovakia and takes it over. The Allies still did nothing.

War in The East

In Asia, The Empire of Japan invades the whole of China. With China splintering into warring factions and communists, Japan starts the war with
relative success. Though later on in the war, the offense slows down as they reach heavy defenses and mountainous terrain.

The War Begins

Germany, with its superior military begins making plans to invade Poland and defend from the Allies until they can properly attack them. Firstly
they sign a Non-Aggression pact with the Soviet Union that includes a plan to split Poland. The planning phases complete before September. Germany
demands the lands lost during the Second World War from Poland. Poland refuses and the Allies say if the Germans attack, they will declare war on them.
The Germans do not listen and invade Poland anyways. The Second World War has begun.
