Wartime: Land Battles

4 Important Land Battles that turned the tide of the war.

The Battle of Moscow

The Battle of Moscow began in the Soviet Union in 1941. This battle was to decide whether the Soviets were to collapse under the weight
of Germany. Germany has 2 million to the Soviets 1.5 million and the Soviets, knowing the terrain and having the weather on their size, win the battle.

The Battle of Stalingrad

Germany was advancing almost unopposed into Southern Russia. Stalingrad was a city that was the most fought for. It suffered more civilian and military
death than the allied casualties combined. This is over 2.5 Million dead. After this battle, the tide turned in favor of the Allies and Soviets.

The Battle of The Bulge

Hitler had a plan to halt the Allied offensive into Germany in 1944 with a surprise blitzkrieg. Germany, with the resources it had, could only keep
it up for only a few days! They also lost over 20,000 men in those few days. After that offensive they lost so much equipment that the defense was even
harder for them! A blunder on Hitlers part. One that cost him any chance of successful defense of Germany.

The Battle of Berlin

Berlin was invaded by the Soviets. Though Germany was outnumbered 5 to 1, it was a very hard fought battle. Hitler's forces fought for every inch of
that city. But in the end they could not hold against the might that was the Soviet Union. When they found Hitler's bunker, they discovered that Hitler commited
suicide. The report of his suicide prompted the forces of Berlin and of Germany to surrender, ending the war in Europe.
